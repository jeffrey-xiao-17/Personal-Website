PennApps

QuincyEncourages

Hosted in the engineering quad at UPenn, PennApps is the nation's largest student-run hackathon. For 3 days, participants work on developing software and solutions to real-world problems. In the end, the weekend provides both a fun academic get-a-way and an incredible learning experience.

In September 2019, I got involved in my first hackathon. Working with 3 friends, we built QuincyEncourages. After reading Blink: The Power of Thinking Without Thinking by Malcolm Gladwell, we pondered on the idea that there can be inherent negative factors to minority groups while taking standardized tests, such as degrading comments. To eliminate this disparity, we created an application that would send positive affirmations to individuals to neutralize any previously toxic influences.

QuincyEncourages is an AI system that sends positive comments via SMS to a student prior to one’s exam to create an encouraging and comfortable environment. The student can schedule one’s assessments via Google calendar, which Quincy will then automatically digest and analyze. Messages are sent based upon the schedule prior to the student’s examinations.
